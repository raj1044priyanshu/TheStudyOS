## Weak topics need structure, not panic

Many students collect a long list of weak areas but do not give those topics a path forward. A useful revision loop turns each weak topic into a sequence: explain it clearly, retrieve it, then schedule the next review.

## Use one main note, not five scattered summaries

A topic is easier to revise when there is one reliable reference. Once that note exists, quizzes and flashcards become much more effective because they point back to the same explanation base.

## Retrieval decides whether the loop is working

If a topic still collapses during recall, the next step is not “try harder.” The next step is a cleaner explanation or another spaced review. That keeps the loop specific and calm.
