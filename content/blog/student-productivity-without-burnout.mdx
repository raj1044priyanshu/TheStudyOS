## More tasks do not mean more progress

Students often overload a schedule because a bigger list feels more serious. In practice, a smaller plan that gets finished creates more trust and momentum.

## Transitions matter

Productivity is not only about the work block itself. It is also about how easily you move into the next useful action. Clear transitions between notes, quizzes, and revision lower the mental cost of starting again.

## Build recovery into the system

Short breaks, realistic session length, and visible stopping points are part of productivity. They are not a reward after productivity happens.
