## Most students do not have a motivation problem

They have a workflow problem. When notes live in one place, quizzes in another, and planning somewhere else, each next step costs more effort than it should.

## Progress compounds when tools connect

If a note can become revision material, if a quiz can trigger follow-up review, and if a plan can reflect actual work, the whole system becomes easier to trust. That trust matters because it removes the need to rebuild the study process every day.

## Calm systems beat noisy systems

A study tool should reduce decision fatigue, not add it. The strongest workflows make the next useful step obvious.
