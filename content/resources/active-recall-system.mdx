## Retrieval should happen close to learning

The best time to test memory is soon after you study, while the structure is still fresh but not effortless. That is why a short quiz or flashcard review after note generation is so effective.

## Use the lightest tool that matches the material

For definitions, formulas, and small facts, flashcards are enough. For chapters, processes, and applied thinking, quizzes reveal more. The goal is not to use every method every day. The goal is to choose the smallest useful form of recall.

## Review mistakes as signals, not failures

A weak score is valuable because it points to what should happen next: revise, simplify, or test again. That keeps recall connected to progress instead of turning it into a scoreboard.

## Keep the system compact

If active recall starts feeling heavy, it stops happening consistently. Keep the loop simple: study, retrieve, review, repeat.
