## Start with constraints, not ambition

Most study plans fail because they begin with an ideal week instead of a real one. Before you decide what to study, define the hours you can actually protect, the subjects that matter most, and the exam dates that create urgency.

## Give each week one clear outcome

Instead of planning ten disconnected goals, pick one measurable outcome for each subject. That might be finishing one chapter, revising one weak area, or completing one quiz-review loop.

## Build three layers into the plan

1. Core study blocks for new learning
2. Retrieval blocks for quizzes and flashcards
3. Revision blocks for material that should stay fresh

That structure keeps planning from turning into a list of content consumption tasks.

## Review the plan at the same time every week

Planning gets stronger when it becomes a rhythm. A short weekly review helps you carry forward unfinished tasks, rebalance weak subjects, and keep upcoming exams visible without rebuilding everything from scratch.
