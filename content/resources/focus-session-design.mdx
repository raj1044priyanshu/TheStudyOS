## Choose one topic, not a vague subject

“Study biology” is too broad. “Review photosynthesis stages” is much more likely to become a complete session. Focus improves when the target becomes specific.

## Match duration to difficulty

Harder problem-solving work usually needs more uninterrupted time. Recall-heavy revision often works in shorter sessions. The session length should fit the work, not the other way around.

## End with a bridge

When a session ends, write down the next logical step. That might be a follow-up topic, a quiz checkpoint, or a quick revision task. This is how one productive session becomes a repeatable pattern instead of an isolated win.
